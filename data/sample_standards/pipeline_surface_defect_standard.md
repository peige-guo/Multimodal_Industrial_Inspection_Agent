# Pipeline Surface Defect Inspection Standard - Demo

## 1. Scope

This demo standard applies to visual inspection of industrial pipeline external surfaces.

## 2. Defect Types

### 2.1 Crack

A crack is a linear discontinuity on the surface. Crack length, width, and location must be recorded.

### 2.2 Corrosion

Corrosion is material degradation indicated by rust, pitting, discoloration, or surface loss.

### 2.3 Scratch

A scratch is a shallow linear surface mark without clear material separation.

## 3. Severity Criteria

### 3.1 Minor Defect

A defect is minor when it is superficial, shorter than 5 mm, not located in a load-bearing or pressure boundary area, and no abnormal sensor reading is present.

### 3.2 Serious Crack

A crack longer than 5 mm located in a load-bearing or pressure boundary area shall be classified as a serious defect. Recommended action: repair or reject pending engineering review.

### 3.3 Critical Sensor Abnormality

If temperature exceeds 80 C, vibration exceeds 10 mm/s, or pressure deviation exceeds 15 percent from baseline, the equipment shall be classified as critical risk. Recommended action: stop machine and perform immediate human inspection.

### 3.4 Corrosion Severity

Corrosion covering more than 10 percent of the inspected local area or showing pitting shall be classified at least as medium severity. If corrosion is near a weld or pressure boundary, classify as high severity.

## 4. Human Review

Human review is mandatory for high or critical severity, missing standard evidence, low model confidence, or conflicting sensor and visual evidence.
